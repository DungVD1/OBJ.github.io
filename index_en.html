OB's Situation Report in the Japanese Market

An Analysis of the Impact of Structural and Strategic Changes

Premise of Establishment: The Reason for the OB JP Production Unit

The OBJP Production Unit was established to meet the specific demands of the Japanese market, focusing on direct interaction and consultation with clients. Based on our experience working in Japan, the realities of the Japanese market, and fierce competition with other outsourcing companies, we recognized the need for people who could understand, work directly with, and consult with clients in Japan. That's why we have SMR.

1. The Current Crisis: Alarming Signs

Personnel Issues

The structural change has caused a wave of resignations, particularly among key personnel:

  • Key member Son resigned, and Mr. Viet changed roles.
  • No new key members were hired after resignations. For example, BRSE reported several months ago that a replacement leader has yet to be hired.
  • Key members at DU and former VJP colleagues reported their resignations because they do not fit with the current organization. After the VJP and VGB merger, a culture clash has emerged, which is not beneficial for members. New leadership lacks member support and a clear career path.

Management Issues

Poor management and risk detection due to a lack of market understanding:

  • No strategic direction for building production capacity to boost sales (e.g., proposals to build internal AI demos to show clients, strategic domain outputs for clients like SAP, healthcare, and insurance; key personnel for areas like Cobol and local Japanese recruitment for farming clients are not being met, leading clients to cancel jobs).

Organizational Issues
