Report on OB's Situation in the Japanese Market

An Analysis of the Impact of Structural Changes and Strategic Direction

Premise of Establishment: The Reason for the OB JP Production Unit

The OB JP production unit was established to meet the specific demands of the Japanese market, focusing on direct interaction and consultation with clients. Based on our experience working with Japan, the realities of the Japanese market, and fierce competition with other outsourcing companies, we recognized the need for people who could understand, work directly with, and consult with clients in Japan. That's why we have SMR.

1. The Current Crisis: Alarming Signs

Personnel Issues

The structural change has caused a wave of resignations, especially among key personnel:

  • Key member Son resigned, and Mr. Viet changed roles.
  • No new key members have been recruited after the resignations. For example, a BRSE told me several months ago that a replacement leader has yet to be hired.
  • Key members at the DU as well as former VJP colleagues have told me they have resigned or plan to resign because they are not a good fit with the current organization. Specifically, after the merger of VJP and VGB, there was a culture clash that was not positive for the members, new leadership lacks support for members, and career paths are unclear.

2. Strategic Missteps: Confusing Business Models

Comparing "Hakken" and "Outsource" Models

Applying a single "profit-centric" strategy to both models is a critical mistake.

Hakken (Staffing)

  • Profit Requirement: Very high
  • Capability Investment: Low
  • Client Relationships: Important, but not decisive

Outsource (Project-based)

  • Profit Requirement: More flexible
  • Capability Investment: Very high initially, then adaptable
  • Client Relationships: Paramount
  • Japanese Client Requirements: Quality and procedure compliance

3. Proposed Solutions: The Way Forward

Strategic Realignment

  • Develop a clear, consistent strategy based on a deep understanding of the Japanese market.
  • Re-evaluate margins so the production unit has room to develop its capabilities.
  • Transfer underqualified PMO personnel to the DU to save costs.

Improving Roles and Responsibilities

  • Need managers who understand the market and have many years of experience in JP.
  • Establish a clear strategy on which business model to focus on.
  • Production unit managers need to join real projects to provide appropriate action.

Ineffective Resource Allocation

A clear strategy on market direction is needed. For the Outsource model, there must be a recruitment mechanism and production personnel costs for OBJP, as this market currently accounts for 40% of VMO's revenue. We should learn from past mistakes, such as the case of VDX, which had low revenue but high recruitment.
